Understanding Burnout

Recognizing the signs and finding your way back to balance.

What is Burnout?

Burnout is a state of emotional, physical, and mental exhaustion caused by excessive and prolonged stress. It occurs when you feel overwhelmed, emotionally drained, and unable to meet constant demands. It's more than just feeling tired; it's a sense of depletion and a loss of motivation that can affect your health and outlook on life.

Recovery and Prevention Strategies

Recovering from burnout requires a conscious effort to rest, reset, and re-evaluate. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Prioritize Rest: Ensure you are getting enough quality sleep and taking regular breaks throughout your day.
  • Set Boundaries: Learn to say "no" to new tasks or commitments when your plate is already full. Protect your time and energy.
  • Disconnect: Schedule time away from screens, email, and work-related thoughts. Allow your mind to truly relax.
  • Reconnect with Hobbies: Make time for activities that bring you joy and fulfillment outside of your responsibilities.

Finding Professional Help

If you're experiencing symptoms of burnout that feel unmanageable, seeking help from a mental health professional is a crucial step. They can help you identify the root causes and develop personalized strategies for recovery.

Here are some resources that can help:

  • Crisis Text Line - Text HOME to 741741
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline - Call or text 988
  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)
